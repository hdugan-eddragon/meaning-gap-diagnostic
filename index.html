Is this a people problem or a system problem?

This 5-minute diagnostic will help you identify whether you're solving the right problem. Bring a real challenge. Get a real answer.

How it works

You'll describe a real performance gap you're facing. The diagnostic will ask you 5 targeted questions. You'll receive a personalized analysis with a specific place to intervene.

Before You Start

What if your people aren't the problem?

Research consistently shows that in most workplace performance gaps, the system is the real culprit — not skill, not effort, not motivation. This diagnostic is built on that assumption. Let's find out what you're actually dealing with.

Research Foundation

This diagnostic is grounded in the research of Baldwin & Ford (learning transfer), Bandura (self-efficacy and behavior change), and Kauffeld et al. (system-level performance support), and is informed by the Meaning Gap Framework developed by Dr. Hope Dugan, Educated Dragon.

Question 4 of 6
Which of these conditions exist in the environment around the work?
Select everything that applies — even if it seems minor.
No protected time to practice or implement People are expected to apply new skills while keeping up with everything else
No feedback loop on performance People don't know if they're doing it right or wrong
It feels risky to try and fail Mistakes are monitored, tracked, or punished rather than used for learning
No visible example of what "good" looks like Training described what to do but not what it actually looks like in practice
Leaders aren't modeling or reinforcing the behavior What leaders do and what they're asking for don't match
No ongoing support after training ended One-and-done training with no coaching, check-ins, or resources after
Competing priorities The new behavior loses every time it's up against existing demands and workload
Tools or resources aren't available People want to do it but don't have what they need to actually do it
No job-embedded practice Learning happened in a room or course — not in the actual flow of work
Incentives are misaligned The reward system — formally or informally — actually punishes the desired behavior
Lack of clarity about expectations Different managers, different messages — nobody really knows what "right" looks like
Change fatigue Too many initiatives, not enough follow-through — people have stopped believing anything will stick
